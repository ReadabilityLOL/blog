Concerning the soup like nature of cereal


December 3, 2025

Abstract

A common question raised, often as either an icebreaker or as a serious matter of contention, is whether or not cereal is a soup. Despite the seemingly simple nature of the question, the answer to whether cereal is a soup is a multifaceted conundrum with real-world implications. In this article, I hope to clarify the various arguments in this field and state my own opinion on the matter.

Introduction

To begin, we must first clearly define the terminology of this field. Cereal, in this article, is defined as a heterogenous substance consisting mostly of a grain-based solid1 submerged in a milk-like liquid2. For the sake of conciseness, the specificities defining grains and milk-like liquids are beyond the scope of this project. A soup is commonly defined as a heterogenous or homogenous substance consisting mostly of a liquid3, sometimes with solids4 contained inside. Along with this, a common extension of the definition of a soup relies on common sense with what is and is not considered a soup. While the previous definition of a soup is often considered complete, the author of this article holds different claims about the definition of a soup that will be revealed later.
